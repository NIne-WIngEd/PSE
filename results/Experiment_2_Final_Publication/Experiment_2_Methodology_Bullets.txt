EXPERIMENT 2 — METHODOLOGY BULLETS
===================================

Dataset and annotation
• The segmentation evaluation used the same finalized 50-image primary set as Experiment 1.
• The class distribution was 13 dots, 7 irregular, 20 lines, and 10 mixed images.
• The original ZIP contained 52 images; dots/dot_12.png and mixed/Mixed_02.png were excluded before final evaluation as development examples.
• Every evaluated image was paired with one externally verified ground-truth mask and one model-predicted mask.
• Ground-truth masks were manually prepared and externally verified using Fiji.
• Foreground pixels were encoded as black = 0 and background pixels as white = 255.
• All 50 image/ground-truth/predicted triplets had matching dimensions.
• All 50 ground-truth masks and all 50 predicted masks were exact binary 0/255 images.
• The model-predicted masks were evaluated at their native image dimensions.

Inference and threshold
• The finalized U-Net segmentation threshold was fixed at 0.5.
• The threshold was not tuned or optimized on the final validation set.
• Predicted masks were not removed or selected according to their final scores.
• The low-performing Mixed_01 case was retained to report model limitations transparently.
• Prediction time recorded in the per-image file was secondary; full runtime benchmarking was conducted separately in Experiment 3.

Pixel-level metrics
• True positives were foreground pixels marked as foreground in both ground truth and prediction.
• False positives were predicted foreground pixels labeled as background in the ground truth.
• False negatives were ground-truth foreground pixels missed by the prediction.
• True negatives were background pixels correctly identified as background.
• Dice was calculated as 2TP / (2TP + FP + FN).
• Intersection over Union was calculated as TP / (TP + FP + FN).
• Pixel precision was calculated as TP / (TP + FP).
• Pixel recall was calculated as TP / (TP + FN).
• Specificity was calculated as TN / (TN + FP).
• Pixel accuracy was calculated as (TP + TN) / (TP + TN + FP + FN).
• Dice and IoU were treated as the primary overlap metrics.
• Pixel accuracy and specificity were interpreted as secondary because background pixels can dominate AFM masks.

Aggregation and uncertainty
• Metrics were calculated separately for every image before summary statistics were computed.
• Overall means therefore weight each image equally rather than pooling all pixels across the dataset.
• The overall 95% confidence intervals used image-level class-stratified percentile bootstrap resampling.
• Class-specific confidence intervals used image-level within-class percentile bootstrap resampling.
• Both bootstrap analyses used 10,000 resamples and random seed 20260622.
• The bootstrap confidence intervals describe uncertainty in the mean image-level metric for this fixed validation set.

Representative and extreme cases
• One representative case per class was selected as the image nearest the class median Dice score.
• The representative images were dots_09, irregular_03, lines_03, and Mixed_04.
• The strongest case was lines_08 with Dice = 0.912 and IoU = 0.838.
• The weakest case was Mixed_01 with Dice = 0.377 and IoU = 0.232.
• Mixed_01 showed substantial under-segmentation: ground-truth foreground fraction = 0.465 and predicted foreground fraction = 0.137.
• Disagreement maps encode true-positive foreground in green, false-positive foreground in red, false-negative foreground in blue, and true-negative background in black.

Primary results
• Mean Dice was 0.845 with 95% bootstrap CI 0.824–0.859.
• Mean IoU was 0.737 with 95% bootstrap CI 0.714–0.754.
• Lines achieved the highest mean Dice score at 0.867.
• Mixed images had the lowest mean Dice score at 0.800 and the greatest variability because of Mixed_01.
• Mean pixel precision was 0.858.
• Mean pixel recall was 0.839.
• Mean specificity was 0.959.
• Mean pixel accuracy was 0.934.

Reporting limitations
• Manual ground-truth segmentation remains subject to annotation uncertainty even after external verification.
• The sample size was modest, especially for irregular images with seven cases.
• Binary overlap metrics do not directly measure boundary distance, topology, or scientific downstream-analysis error.
• The severe Mixed_01 outlier materially widens uncertainty for the mixed class and should not be removed post hoc.
• Results apply to the evaluated rendered AFM images and do not establish performance on all instruments, scan settings, materials, or color-rendering domains.
