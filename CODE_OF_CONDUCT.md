# Code of Conduct

## 1. Purpose

The AFM Morphology Analysis Platform is a research-software project intended to support reproducible, ethical, and properly credited scientific work. Everyone who participates in this repository—including contributors, users, reviewers, collaborators, and maintainers—is expected to act professionally, respectfully, and honestly.

This Code of Conduct applies to repository discussions, issues, pull requests, code reviews, documentation, releases, research outputs, presentations, publications, and other public or private interactions connected to this project.

---

## 2. Expected Behavior

Participants are expected to:

- communicate respectfully and constructively;
- provide technically honest feedback;
- document changes and sources clearly;
- distinguish original work from reused or adapted work;
- respect intellectual-property, copyright, license, and data-use restrictions;
- preserve the scientific record and avoid misleading claims;
- report errors, conflicts of interest, security concerns, and research-integrity concerns promptly;
- acknowledge contributors and prior work fairly;
- avoid uploading confidential, proprietary, restricted, or personally identifiable data without authorization.

---

## 3. Strict Policy Against Plagiarism and Misappropriation

Plagiarism, unattributed reuse, false authorship, and misrepresentation of this work are strictly prohibited.

No person may copy, reproduce, adapt, redistribute, publish, submit, present, sell, commercialize, or otherwise use substantial portions of this repository while falsely representing the material as their own or concealing its original source.

This prohibition applies to, without limitation:

- source code and scripts;
- model architectures and implementation details;
- trained model weights;
- figures, tables, graphs, and captions;
- manual or predicted masks;
- evaluation results and benchmark data;
- documentation and written explanations;
- experimental designs and workflows;
- downstream-analysis outputs;
- repository structure and interface designs;
- substantial derivative works based on this project.

The following conduct is explicitly unacceptable:

1. **Copying without attribution**  
   Reusing material from this repository without preserving required notices and clearly acknowledging the original project and authors.

2. **False claims of authorship or ownership**  
   Claiming that this software, its results, or its research contributions were created independently when they were copied, adapted, or derived from this repository.

3. **Academic plagiarism**  
   Submitting repository content, results, figures, code, documentation, or derivative work for a course, thesis, publication, competition, grant, fellowship, conference, or research evaluation without proper citation and disclosure.

4. **Professional or commercial misrepresentation**  
   Using this work in a portfolio, job application, product, consulting service, startup, commercial offering, or client deliverable while hiding its origin or exaggerating personal contribution.

5. **Removal of attribution or provenance**  
   Deleting or obscuring copyright notices, license notices, citations, authorship information, commit history, acknowledgments, or other provenance records.

6. **Uncredited derivative work**  
   Modifying, translating, reorganizing, or extending this project and then presenting the derivative as entirely original.

7. **Fabricated contribution claims**  
   Claiming authorship, co-authorship, maintainership, institutional ownership, or research credit without a documented contribution.

8. **Misuse of reviewer access**  
   Using material accessed during confidential peer review for personal, academic, competitive, or commercial benefit, or redistributing it outside the review process.

Proper attribution must be clear, visible, and reasonably placed. Acknowledgment hidden in source history, an obscure dependency file, or an inaccessible webpage is not sufficient when the reused material is presented publicly.

At a minimum, users must:

- comply with the repository's `LICENSE`;
- preserve all copyright and license notices required by that license;
- identify this repository as the source of reused or adapted material;
- clearly state when changes have been made;
- cite the associated repository release and software article in academic work when citation information is available;
- avoid implying endorsement, partnership, or authorship by the original maintainers;
- provide the complete corresponding source code when distribution or network deployment triggers that obligation under the AGPL;
- keep a clear source-code offer or link available to remote users of a modified network-accessible deployment when required by AGPL section 13.

A recommended attribution format is:

> This work uses or adapts the AFM Morphology Analysis Platform by Mostakim Khan Rayan, available at https://github.com/NIne-WIngEd/PSE.

For academic publications, users should also follow the citation instructions in `CITATION.cff`.

## 4. Relationship to the AGPL License

This Code of Conduct does not replace, narrow, expand, or modify the rights and obligations in the repository's software license.

The project is licensed under the **GNU Affero General Public License, version 3 or later** (`AGPL-3.0-or-later`). The `LICENSE` file governs the legal permissions and conditions for use, copying, modification, distribution, and network deployment.

The AGPL permits commercial use. Commercial use by itself is not misconduct. However, users must comply with all AGPL requirements, including preserving notices, providing corresponding source code when required, licensing covered derivative works under the AGPL, and offering corresponding source to users who interact remotely with a modified network-deployed version as required by section 13.

Permission to reuse software under the AGPL does **not** permit plagiarism, false authorship, removal of provenance, misleading contribution claims, or concealment of the original source.

This Code of Conduct establishes ethical, scientific-integrity, attribution, and community expectations. It must not be interpreted as imposing additional restrictions on rights granted by the AGPL.

## 5. Research Integrity

Participants must not:

- fabricate, falsify, selectively alter, or misrepresent results;
- manipulate images or masks in a way that is not disclosed;
- omit known failures or exclusions in order to create a misleading impression;
- claim that reconstructed training scripts are the exact historical scripts;
- claim performance beyond the validated dataset and documented scope;
- claim reductions in human analysis time without a supporting human-workflow study;
- redistribute AFM images or other data without confirmed permission;
- use confidential reviewer materials outside the review process.

Corrections should be made transparently. Material changes to methods, results, model files, or evaluation data should be documented in commits, releases, or correction notes.

## 6. Respectful Participation

Unacceptable interpersonal conduct includes:

- harassment, discrimination, intimidation, or personal attacks;
- threats or incitement of violence;
- sexualized language or unwanted sexual attention;
- deliberate disruption of discussions or reviews;
- publishing another person's private information without permission;
- retaliating against someone who reports a concern in good faith;
- knowingly making false accusations.

Technical disagreement is permitted and encouraged when expressed professionally and supported by evidence.

---

## 7. Reporting Violations

Concerns involving plagiarism, attribution, research integrity, harassment, security, or misuse should be reported privately to the project maintainer whenever possible.

Maintainer:

**Mostakim Khan Rayan**  
GitHub: [NIne-WIngEd](https://github.com/NIne-WIngEd)

Reports should include, when available:

- a description of the conduct;
- links, screenshots, files, or commit references;
- the date and location of the incident;
- the names or usernames involved;
- any steps already taken;
- whether confidentiality is requested.

Do not publish sensitive evidence or confidential reviewer material in a public issue. A private GitHub security advisory or direct contact through the maintainer's GitHub profile may be used when appropriate.

Good-faith reports will be reviewed as promptly and fairly as possible.

---

## 8. Enforcement

Depending on severity, frequency, evidence, and impact, maintainers may take one or more of the following actions:

- request correction, citation, or attribution;
- require removal of copied or misleading material;
- reject or close an issue or pull request;
- revert a contribution;
- issue a private or public warning;
- temporarily or permanently restrict participation;
- ban a user from project spaces;
- preserve evidence of the violation;
- notify GitHub or another hosting platform;
- notify a journal, conference, university, employer, funding body, competition organizer, or other relevant institution;
- submit a copyright, license, or plagiarism complaint;
- pursue other remedies available under the license or applicable law.

Serious plagiarism, deliberate false authorship, misuse of confidential review material, or repeated removal of attribution may result in immediate permanent exclusion and external reporting without a prior warning.

Enforcement decisions will be based on documented evidence. Knowingly false or malicious reports may themselves violate this Code of Conduct.

## 9. Scope
